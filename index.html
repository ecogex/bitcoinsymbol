Why Ƀ?

Why the Bitcoin community should definitely addopt this symbol?
Since the birth of Bitcoin, a large number of logos and symbols where introduced. The most popular is a bold falling serif B, crossed by two vertical dashes à la dollar.

The problem with the image on the left is: it’s a logo! Just like a logo for a company, or used to sell or promote a product. Currencies are symbolised by signs like $, € or ¥, that can be used everywhere by everybody. The Thai Baht (฿) is sometimes used as a Bitcoin sign, but the confusion with the currency of Thailand is inevitable.

The idea to use Ƀ for Bitcoin is not new, the character was usually part of the candidates to represent Bitcoin. But it was most of the time used to design a logo for Bitcoin. A logo means a centralised graphic identity: colors, fonts, shapes chosen by a small group of individals. But Bitcoin is a decentralised currency not a brand, a product nor a company, and what we need to reprensent Bitcoin is a symbol.

Ƀ is not a logo but a symbol: Unicode Character U+0243 can be used in every texts.

This unicode character is originally used as a phonetic symbol to represent to transcribe the sound [β]. The context of this use does not allow any confusion with the Bitcoin currency.

FAQ

Is this free?
Yes, all the content you can download in the graphic pack is free to use, copy, distribute, and transform. Our work is under the Free Art Licence. We make no salary from this project and we give away all the work we do for free. If you like it, you can support our work by different maners.
It’s a hack! Why not ask the unicode consortium to add a brand new character?
We think that there is nothing better than a hack to represent Bitcoin. Bitcoin creators and users are hackers, this is part of the project’s DNA. As there is no Bitcoin consortium, agreeing on a new character to use may be dificult and may take a very long time.
