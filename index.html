Bitcoin deserves the right symbol.

Why Ƀ?

Why the Bitcoin community should addopt this symbol? Since the begining of Bitcoine, a large number of logos and symbols where introduced. The most popular is a bold falling serif B, crossed by two vertical dashes à la dollar.

Currencies are represented by signs like $ € ¥. that can be used everywhere. The Thai Baht (฿) is sometimes used as a Bitcoin sign, but the confusion with the currency of Thailand is inevitable.

The idea to use Ƀ for Bitcoin is not new, the character was usually part of the candidates to represent Bitcoin. But it was most of the time to design a logo for Bitcoin. As you may know, Bitcoin is a decentralised P2P currency, there is no reason for its identity to be centralised as a logo. What we need to reprensent Bitcoin is a symbol.

Not a logo but a symbol: Unicode Character U+0243 can be used in every texts.

This unicode character is originally used as a phonetic symbol to represent to transcribe the sound [β]. The context of this use does not allow any confusion with the Bitcoin currency.
